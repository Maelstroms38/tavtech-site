ABOUT TAVhacks

We believe in impact. We have convened young technologists and entrepreneurs from all over the world in Tel Aviv from NYU, Brown, Cornell, Harvard, MIT, and 26 other college campuses and 7 countries to ignite grassroots social change.


Diverse, technical entrepreneurs. Competitors in the hackathon come from a variety of academic backgrounds in cybersecurity, data science, big data, artificial intelligence, and programming - as well as non-technical fields.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is best described as an “invention marathon”. Anyone who has an interest in technology attends a hackathon to learn, build and share their creations over the course of a weekend in a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. You don’t have to be a programmer and you certainly don’t have to be majoring in Computer Science. (Major League Hacking definition)

How many people can be in a team?

We strongly recommend teams of 3-5 people, but will accept teams of less than, or of at most 6 people.

Do I need a team or idea to sign up?

Teams must be formed prior to hackathon start and submitted to organizer via form by Tuesday, January 16th at 5pm.

All projects must start from scratch and built during the time you are at the event. Ideas may be generated beforehand or during TAVhacks.

Who can participate?

All TAVtech Fellows and students at The College of Management Academic Studies can participate.

How does judging work?

Judging will be based on four criterion: Potential Impact, Quality of Idea, Design, and Function. An elaboration will be provided prior to the event to those who have signed up.

I have more questions. Who should I contact?

Please send additional questions to hackathon@taventure.org.
Happy hacking!

Who We Are

TAVtech Ventures Global (TAVtech) is a nonprofit that operates a coding and entrepreneurship fellowship which brings students from top US universities to Israel over winter break to learn high demand coding skills while being immersed in Israel’s startup economy.
